Parks

To find your nearest park go to Google and type "parks" the top result will be a map with the nearest parks.

Parks are places that communities in built-up areas can go to exercise. Most parks consist of some kind of playground that children can use, these have things like monkey bars and swings which all aid in children’s physical activity. Parks also typically consist open expanses of grass where children can play sports. Some parks even have soccer goals and football posts, children can go to the parks with friends and engage in sports to get their recommended amount of physical activity each day. 

Sports

Sports are a critical factor in helping children reach the recommended amount of daily physical activity. Most schools offer some sort of extra curricular sporting opportunities for the students, however there are also options to enroll your child(s) in sporting clubs to partake in sports with the broader community. Even in big cities, sports are very popular activites and clubs make use of their fields for training and competition days. Inter-school and club sports are also an excellent way for your child to make friends and have a level of social interaction that they would otherwise not experience in their classroom or home environment. Team and individual sports help children become physically fit, which can have numerous health benefits such as; less risk of Type 2 diabetes, lowering blood cholesterol levels, improved cardio-respiratory system and longer life expectancy.

Outdoor toys

Having space to play is a major factor in a child’s physical development. Houses with backyards can be converted into an exciting environment where children can play and develop gross motor and locomotor skills with the help of things like; trampolines, swing-sets, monkey bars, hula-hoops balls, hoops and climbing frames. Children exert a tremendous amount of energy using these things and it can help them reach their physical activity goal when they are not participating in sports or other recreational activities.

Indoor activities

If none of the options above are available or it is a rainy day, there are many activities that can be done from the comfort of your own home. Martial arts, dancing and games like “Twister” or “The Floor is Lava” are great ways to get your children active inside, without worrying about the children breaking anything due to spacial limitations. There are also gaming consoles that provide active games such as the Nintendo Wii and the Xbox Kinect. The Nintendo Wii has a controller that is used through hand and arm movements. The Xbox Kinect is similar however, it uses the player’s own body as a controller. There are many games which can be purchased for these consoles such as sports games and “Just Dance”, a high-energy dancing game that requires the player to move their body to the beat of the song.
