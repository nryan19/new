The importance of fitness

Being physically fit enables a person to live life with less hindrance of health issues that stem from inactivity. By partaking in at least 30 minutes of physical exercise a day, you can reduce risk of; heart attack, type 2 diabetes, excessive weight gain, high blood cholesterol levels and you can improve your mental state by relieving stress and enhancing your concentration and mood. Exercising is also a great way to get outdoors and meet new people, it can help decrease the risk of depression due to social isolation and provide opportunities to participate in sports as an individual or part of a team. Research conducted by the Australian Bureau of Statistics revealed that the average amount of physical activity that young children and adolescents partake in is far from the recommended daily amount of 60 minutes per day (Queensland Department of Health, 2014). The statistics are as follows:


Children aged between 2 and 4 years spent 6 hours per day participating in physical activity. They also spent an average of hour and a half each day on some form of technology according to the Australian Health Survey: Physical Activity 2011-2012 (ABS).

Children aged 5-17 spent only one and a half hours participating in physical activity and more than two hours each day using technology.
Physical inactivity (low levels of physical activity) is the fourth leading cause of death due to non-communicable disease (NCDs) worldwide (heart disease, stroke, diabetes and cancers) – contributing to over three million preventable deaths annually (6% of deaths globally). (Queensland Department of Heatlh, 2014).

 Physical inactivity is estimated to be the main cause for approximately 21–25% of breast and colon cancers, 27% of diabetes and approximately 30% of ischaemic heart disease cases. (Queensland Department of Health, 2014).
Physical inactivity is the second greatest contributor, behind tobacco smoking, to the cancer burden in Australia. (Queensland Department of Health, 2014).
